At the beginning of the experiment, you will be randomly matched with another participant.

The computer will then randomly assign a role to each of you. One participant will be Participant A and the other one will be Participant B. These labels will ensure that neither of you know the identity of the other participant but you will only know your own roles.

The experiment works as follows:

  1. Participant A sees 10 cards with a question mark. Behind each card, there is a color. 8 of the cards are black and 2 are orange. The cards are placed in a random order. Participant A clicks on a card and the card flips and shows the color behind it.
  2. As soon as Participant A sees the color behind the picked card, she or he reports the color of the picked card. A message will later be sent to Participant B informing than of the color reported by Participant A.
  3. Then, Participant B sees another set of 10 cards with a question mark. Behind each card, there is a color. 8 of the cards are black and 2 are orange. The cards are placed in a random order. Participant B clicks on a card and the card flips and shows the color behind it.
  4. After picking a card, Participant B receives a message which informs her or him about the color reported by Participant A.
  5. Next, the computer automatically reports the color behind the card picked by Participant B. That is, unlike Participant A, Participant B will not report what she or she saw but the computer will submit the color picked.
  6. Finally, Participant B will report the color of the card she or he picked.

The colors reported by Participant A and the computer determine the payments in the experiment for Participant A. If Participant A or the computer reports an orange card (no matter who), Participant A receive {{Constants.payoff_win}}. If both reports are of a black card, Participant A receive {{Constants.payoff_lose}}.

The payment for Participant B only depends on her or his drawn card. Participant B receives {{Constants.payoff_win}} if he or she picks an orange card, and {{Constants.payoff_lose}} if he or she picks a black card. The possible combinations are summarized in the table below.

There will be no further rounds in this experiment. That is, you will participate in the task described above only once.
