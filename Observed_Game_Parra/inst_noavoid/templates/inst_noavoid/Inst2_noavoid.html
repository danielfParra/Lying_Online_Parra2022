At the beginning of the experiment, you will be randomly matched with another participant.

The computer will then randomly assign a role to each of you. One participant will be Participant A and the other one will be Participant B. These labels will ensure that neither of you know the identity of the other participant but you will only know your own roles.

The experiment works as follows:

  1. Participant A sees 10 cards with a question mark. Behind each card, there is a color. 8 of the cards are black and 2 are orange. The cards are placed in a random order. Participant A clicks on a card and the card flips and shows the color behind it. The color of the picked card is only known by Participant A.
  2. As soon as Participant A sees the color behind the picked card, she or he reports the color of the picked card. A message will later be sent to Participant B informing than of the color reported by Participant A.
  3. Then, Participant B sees a new set of 10 cards with a question mark. Behind each card, there is a color. 8 of the cards are black and 2 are orange. The cards are placed in a random order. Participant B clicks on a card and the card flips and shows the color behind it.
  4. After picking a card, Participant B receives the message with color reported by Participant A.
  5. Next, the computer automatically reports the color behind the card picked by Participant B. That is, unlike Participant A, Participant B will not report what she or he saw but the computer will do so on behalf of Participant B.

The colors reported by Participant A and by the computer (on behalf of Participant B) determine the payments in the experiment. If either Participant A or the computer report an orange card (no matter who), both participants receive {{Constants.payoff_win}}. If both reports are of a black card, both participants receive {{Constants.payoff_lose}}. The possible combinations are summarized in the table below.
